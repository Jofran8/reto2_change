ABOUT THE TELESCOPE

The James Webb Space Telescope is a space observatory developed through the collaboration of twenty countries, built and operated jointly by NASA, the European Space Agency and the Canadian Space Agency, to replace the Hubble and Spitzer telescopes. JWST's scientific mission has four main goals: to find light from the first stars and galaxies that formed in the universe after the Big Bang; study the formation and evolution of galaxies; understand the formation of stars and solar systems; and study planetary systems and the origins of life.

16 Feb 2022 Studying the Next Interstellar Interloper with Webb

One of the most exciting findings in planetary science in recent years is the discovery of interstellar objects passing through our solar system. So far, astronomers have confirmed only two of these interlopers from other star systems — 1I/'Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2018 — but many, many more are thought to exist.

NASA’s Dr. Aprille Ericsson has received the Ralph Coats Roe medal from the American Society for Mechanical Engineers (ASME), honoring her international work encouraging young people, women, and people from other underrepresented groups to pursue STEM careers.
